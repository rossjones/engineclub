The Living Well @ the Library is a service developed by Renfrewshire Libraries and other local organisations to provide community health and wellbeing information local to Renfrewshire.

Volunteers provide health and wellbeing information at drop-in sessions in local libraries. This site is part of the service, powered by the ALISS engine, an initiative supported by Long Term Conditions Alliance Scotland and Scottish Government.

The ALISS project aims to make information about local community services easier to find, with a particular focus on self management support for people living with long term conditions. The ALISS Engine indexes links to useful local support and makes those links openly available, liberating them from local data stores that are sometimes hard to find or access.

This site only searches the links that the LW@TL community partners have contributed and curated, ensuring that results are rich, local, relevant and current.

The Living well @ the Library application allows information and resources in health and wellbeing services to be signposted and shared in your community.

The information is formed from existing data and newly added data. The Living Well @the Library community find, add and signpost a range of resources into the community that they regard as being useful and valuable.

The inclusion of an item does not imply endorsement or promotion of that resource or service from the Living Well @the Library application.

There is no guarantee made that the information contained in Living Well @the Library application is accurate, complete and current at any given time. It may also change at any time.

The Living well @the Library application is not intended in any way to replace the advice from a doctor or any other health care professional. Go to a qualified health professional if you have specific concerns about your health.
